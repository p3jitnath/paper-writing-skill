# Writing the Discussion

## Move 1: Synthesize the answer

Restate the scientific answer without repeating the Results section numerically.

## Move 2: Synthesize mechanisms

Connect the local interpretations already established in Results to governing physical relationships. Compare supported mechanisms with plausible alternatives; do not postpone all interpretation until this section.

## Move 3: Situate the result

Explain how the finding changes current weather/climate modeling, forecasting, or understanding and where it agrees or conflicts with prior work.

## Move 4: Partition uncertainty

Discuss sampling, observations, initial conditions, internal variability, model structure, forcing/scenario, and methodological uncertainty as applicable.

## Move 5: State limitations by consequence

Identify which conclusions, domains, regimes, resolutions, lead times, or uses are limited. Distinguish missing evidence from known failure.

## Move 6: Define the next decisive test

Propose the experiment or observation that would resolve the most important remaining uncertainty. Avoid generic future-work lists.

Results and Discussion may be combined when the genre or venue benefits from immediate process interpretation. Preserve the same distinction between observation, local interpretation, synthesis, and limitation.
