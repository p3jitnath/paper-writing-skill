# Writing Results and Verification

## Move 1: Anchor the question

Open each subsection with the scientific question and the evidence that answers it.

## Move 2: Establish the reference comparison

Report the primary result against the named reference with metric, domain, period, lead time, and uncertainty.

## Move 3: Disaggregate

Show where the result strengthens or fails across lead time, region, level, season, regime, intensity, and event type.

## Move 4: Verify probabilistic and physical behavior

Assess calibration and distributional quality for probabilistic outputs. Test budgets, spectra, teleconnections, stability, or process diagnostics required by physical claims.

For ensemble forecasts, distinguish ensemble-mean accuracy, marginal proper scores and spread–skill behavior, and joint spatial-temporal coherence. State which uncertainty sources are represented, test ensemble-size sensitivity, and inspect whether calibration-oriented training introduces smoothing, noise, or structural artifacts.

For limited-area forecasts, report interior-domain skill separately from supplied boundary cells and distinguish reference-boundary experiments from operational global-model boundary forcing.

For observation-to-forecast systems, report lead-zero state-estimation error separately from forecast-error growth, then test modality ablations, outages, coverage and latency changes, sensor/platform shift, and unseen locations. For task- or region-specific end-to-end tuning, show both the targeted gain and non-target trade-offs.

For learned model components, distinguish offline prediction quality from coupled online/prognostic behavior and report conflicts between short-range skill and long-run climate or stability.

For climate emulators, separate prescribed-boundary and interactive-coupling results. Progress from stability and mean bias to vertical structure, variability across timescales, budgets, coupled modes, and controlled forcing responses. Compare errors with the internal-variability floor where available.

## Move 5: Sensitivity and alternatives

Report ablations, resolution and data sensitivity, seed or ensemble variability, and plausible alternative explanations.

For mechanism papers, test the proposed constraint against the competing explanation and identify the regime where its assumptions weaken. For impact papers, report each link from the physical response to the event distribution and nonlinear consequence; do not let a basin or global aggregate stand in for tail risk.

## Move 6: Bounded takeaway

State exactly what the evidence establishes and what it does not. Do not use aggregate score gains to claim mechanism, climate credibility, or operational readiness.

Interpret the result locally: explain the physical or methodological reason supported by this diagnostic and name important exceptions. Leave only cross-result synthesis and competing explanations for Discussion.
