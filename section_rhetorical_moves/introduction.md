# Writing the Introduction

Write Introduction 0 before finalizing experiments, then rewrite the final introduction after Results and Discussion stabilize.

## Move 1: Scientific stakes

Name the Earth-system phenomenon, scale, and consequence. Prefer a concrete forecast, modeling, or understanding failure over generic climate urgency.

## Move 2: Knowledge or capability gap

Explain what current physical, statistical, or ML approaches cannot establish and why. Distinguish missing accuracy, missing physical credibility, missing resolution, excessive cost, and missing understanding.

## Move 3: Core insight

State the physical or methodological insight that makes progress possible. Do not lead with the model name.

## Move 4: Approach

Explain how the data, model, or diagnostic operationalizes the insight. Include the task, scale, and evidence boundary.

## Move 5: Contributions as findings

State contributions as claims with evidence. Separate predictive, physical, computational, and operational contributions instead of allowing one metric to imply all four.

## Move 6: Results preview and limits

Give the main result with metric, reference, domain, period, and uncertainty needed for interpretation. State the most consequential scope limit when omission would mislead.

## Venue adaptation

Earth-science journals usually foreground the scientific question and physical interpretation. ML conferences require a sharper method distinction and controlled experimental contribution. Preserve the same evidence boundary in both tracks.
