# Dennis Hartmann Voice Profile: Climate Dynamics

Use for climate variability, feedbacks, cloud-radiative processes, circulation, climate sensitivity, and mechanism-centered analysis.

## Reasoning pattern

- Start from a physical climate question and identify the governing balance, feedback, or dynamical relationship.
- When a simple constraint organizes the paper, state the observed regularity, trace the constraint to its controlling physics, test it in the smallest adequate model hierarchy, and then derive its climate implication.
- Move from the observed or simulated pattern to a small set of mechanism diagnostics.
- Separate forced response, internal variability, and uncertainty before interpreting trends or differences.
- Test alternative physical explanations instead of treating correlation or feature importance as mechanism.
- Use AI as an analytical or modeling instrument; keep the climate process as the grammatical and intellectual subject.

## Prose pattern

- Prefer compact declarative sentences and restrained claims.
- Introduce figures by the physical relationship they test, not by panel order alone.
- Quantify the pattern, then explain how it affects an energy, moisture, momentum, or circulation balance.
- Use calibrated language when observations, models, or diagnostics cannot uniquely identify a mechanism.
- In a short letter, allow the section sequence to follow hypothesis → model test → result → implication instead of expanding it into full IMRaD.

## Red-team questions

- Is the claimed mechanism supported by a budget, perturbation, process diagnostic, or convergent evidence?
- Could internal variability, sampling, or a shared model bias explain the pattern?
- Are feedback sign, normalization, reference state, and radiative convention explicit?
- Does the conclusion exceed the represented models, scenarios, or observational record?

This profile is a synthesis of recurring expository traits in Hartmann's climate publications; do not reuse distinctive wording. See `references/voice_sources.md` for the source corpus and derivation boundary.
