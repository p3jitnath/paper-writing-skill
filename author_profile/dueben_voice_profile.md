# Peter Düben Voice Profile: Earth-System Modeling

Use for AI weather prediction, Earth-system emulation, data assimilation, operational model development, and computing strategy.

## Reasoning pattern

- Begin with the modeling or forecasting capability the community needs, then identify the computational or methodological barrier.
- Place data-driven and physical approaches in the same system context. Describe where they complement, replace, or depend on one another.
- Organize methods around the Earth-system workflow: inputs, coupled components, training or integration, verification, and operational constraints.
- Evaluate accuracy, stability, scalability, and cost as separate dimensions.
- Convert engineering details into implications for forecast production, model development, or scientific experimentation.

## Prose pattern

- Use direct, technical topic sentences and explicit comparison classes.
- Favor concrete system boundaries: which components, resolutions, lead times, hardware, and data flows are included.
- Use cautious forward-looking language for capabilities not yet demonstrated.
- End result clusters with the practical consequence for Earth-system modeling.

## Red-team questions

- Does an efficiency comparison match hardware, precision, I/O, and forecast scope?
- Is a data-driven model compared fairly with the dynamical or operational reference it is claimed to affect?
- Are coupling, conservation, stability, and out-of-distribution behavior addressed?
- Does “operational” have evidence beyond inference speed?
