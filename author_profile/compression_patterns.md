# Compression Patterns

Compress only after the scientific argument stabilizes.

1. Delete generic motivation after the specific phenomenon and consequence are established.
2. Merge repeated dataset descriptions into one authoritative Data subsection and cross-reference it.
3. Move long architecture inventories, hyperparameters, and secondary diagnostics to a supplement or table.
4. Replace prose lists of numerical results with a figure or table, keeping one interpretive sentence.
5. State the dominant result first, then the uncertainty and important exception.
6. Remove repeated definitions while preserving units and the distinction among observations, reanalyses, and simulations.
7. Consolidate limitations by consequence: validity, transfer, physical interpretation, operational use, and reproducibility.

Never compress away the reference forecast, evaluation period, uncertainty, physical caveat, data provenance, or failure regime needed to interpret a claim.
