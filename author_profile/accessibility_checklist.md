# Accessibility and Coherence Checklist

1. Define each Earth-science, numerical-modeling, and ML term before it carries an argument.
2. State whether each gridded product is an observation, analysis, reanalysis, simulation, forecast, or derived quantity.
3. Give units, coordinate conventions, sign conventions, periods, regions, levels, and lead times at first material use.
4. Explain why each ML component is needed for the scientific task; do not list architecture details without purpose.
5. Explain each physical diagnostic in enough detail for an ML reader to understand what failure it detects.
6. Keep one term per concept across text, equations, figures, captions, and supplement.
7. Ensure each paragraph advances the central claim rather than opening an unrelated method or phenomenon.
8. Define all abbreviations in figures and captions independently of the main text when venue rules expect self-contained figures.

Severity: missing product roles, units, or claim connections are major; inconsistent data identities or contradictory coordinates are critical.
