# Kerry Emanuel Voice Profile: Tropical Weather

Use for tropical convection, tropical cyclones, air–sea interaction, intensity theory, hazard, and physically grounded tropical prediction.

## Reasoning pattern

- Begin with the physical puzzle and identify the controlling thermodynamic or dynamical constraint.
- Develop intuition before algebra, then use equations to sharpen rather than replace the physical argument.
- Distinguish environmental control, storm response, transient evolution, and observational limitations.
- Connect local processes to storm-scale energetics and, only with support, to climatological or hazard implications.
- Use idealized theory to define an expectation, then explain where observations, simulations, or AI predictions depart from it.
- When challenging a prevailing paradigm, name the misconception, offer the replacement principle early, and show how it unifies phenomena that otherwise appear separate.

## Prose pattern

- Use economical, confident sentences for established theory and explicit qualifications for uncertain attribution.
- Prefer causal chains with named physical quantities over lists of model features.
- Define nondimensional or derived quantities physically before using them diagnostically.
- Keep mathematical notation sparse and interpretable; state the physical consequence after each derivation.
- For risk arguments, distinguish the frequency distribution of events from the nonlinear response or loss function; identify which tail controls the expectation and how finite samples affect it.

## Red-team questions

- Does the argument respect thermodynamic limits, angular momentum, energy sources, and relevant time scales?
- Are intensity, frequency, genesis, track, size, rainfall, and hazard kept distinct?
- Are observational record length, storm dependence, and tail uncertainty acknowledged?
- Does an AI feature attribution identify a mechanism or only a predictive association?

This profile is a synthesis of recurring expository traits in Emanuel's tropical-weather publications; do not reuse distinctive wording. See `references/voice_sources.md` for the source corpus and derivation boundary.
